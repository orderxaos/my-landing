About

Hi, I'm Fedor. I'm a Russian born designer that has called New York City home for over two decades.

I believe design was built into me early on. My father, an architect, would point out things it would be easy to overlook for a child—like a rare marble on a building, imported from a specific quarry in Italy. Details like that made the world feel bigger to me, and they sharpened how I saw it. From an early age, I learned that the built world isn't accidental: it's chosen, crafted, and deeply considered.

Today, I call myself a product designer, but that's only part of the story. I've been a visual designer, illustrator, UXer, strategist, occasional front-end tinkerer, and design lead. What ties it all together is a love for curiosity, clarity, usability, and just the right measure of intrigue—because simple should never mean bland, and functional should never mean joyless.

I take inspiration from Dieter Rams' principles of good design, but I may double down on elevating the everyday. Order is essential, chaos inevitable—the craft lies in making sense without rendering it sterile.

Process

While most design processes focus on deliverables and features, I've adopted a hybrid approach that combines the best of Design Thinking and Lean UX. I use Design Thinking for ideation but ground the overall process in Lean UX principles that prioritize outcomes over outputs. This methodology transforms assumptions into validated solutions through the Think → Make → Check cycle, shifting teams away from building what we think users want to validating what they actually need.

Instead of starting with requirements (which we often mistake for facts), this process begins with assumptions—acknowledging that we might be wrong and building in mechanisms to course-correct quickly. Using collaborative tools like the Lean UX Canvas and hypothesis-driven design, I guide cross-functional teams through structured exercises that align everyone around shared outcomes rather than individual deliverables.
